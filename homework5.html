Independence and Uncorrelation

Two random variables XXX and YYY are Uncorrelated when their correlation coefficent is 0.ρ(X,Y)=Cov[X,Y]Var[X]⋅Var[Y]=0 ρ(X,Y) = \frac{Cov[X,Y]}{\sqrt{Var[X] \cdot Var[Y]}} = 0 ρ(X,Y)=Var[X]⋅Var[Y]​Cov[X,Y]​=0
XXX and YYY are Independent when Pr[X=x∧Y=y]=Pr[X=x]⋅Pr[Y=y]Pr[X=x ∧ Y=y] = Pr[X=x] \cdot Pr[Y=y]Pr[X=x∧Y=y]=Pr[X=x]⋅Pr[Y=y]. For a more in depth explanation of independence, see here
Independence is a stronger property in respect to Uncorellation. In fact, Independence implies Uncorrellation, but the opposite is not true.
Uncorrelation just means that the two variables are not linearly related, but it says nothing about other types of possible non linear relations.
Another more intuitive way of saying that two variables are linearly related is saying that the two variables visualized on a graph form a more or less straight line.
